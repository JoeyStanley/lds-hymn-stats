On June 12th, 2025, the church released the fourth batch of new hymns. In previous posts, I have covered in detail the first, second, and third batches of hymns and how they were incorporated into sacrament meetings. This page covers period since when the fourth batch came out and will update weekly as more data comes in.

Currently I have data from 8,251 sacrament meetings from 1,101 wards since June 15, 2025. Here’s how much data I have for each week so far, just so you have an idea of what I’m working with.

How many wards sang new hymns each week?

The following plot show what percentage of wards sang from each of the new batches of hymns, per week, since June 2024 when the new hymns started getting rolled out. I’ve included data from all batches just so you can compare them to each other.

Ignoring unknown labels:
• fill : "date"

Since the fourth batch was released, we’ve seen a very slow rise in popularity. August 24th was the best so far with 6.7% of wards singing from it. All four batches have been pretty consistent since about then, with the first batch generally the most common, then the second, then the third, then the fourth. We are now to the point though where just about half of wards are singing at least one new hymn in a given week.

Here’s another version of the same plot but instead of the actual dates, it’s in terms of how many weeks since the batches came out. This version of the plot is useful to spot any parallel trends across the batches, especially in the first few weeks of their release.

Ignoring unknown labels:

Here we can see that each batch is a little less popular than the previous one, both overall and week by week. Nearly 20% of wards sang from the first batch the week it came out. About 8% did for the second batch. About 1% for the third batch. And of the 576 wards I have data from on June 15th, just one of them sang from the fourth batch. Granted, it was Father’s Day, but after almost two months, still only a few wards have sung from this batch. For the fourth batch, it has never been as popular as any of the others until recently where it has surpassed the second batch at this point since their respective releases. Although that’s probably because the second batch was not super popular during Christmastime.

It’s perhaps not too surprising that the most popular one is O Lord, Who Gave Thy Life for Me (#1041) since it’s a sacrament hymn. For a while, I’m a Pioneer Too (#1034) was the #1 hymn in this batch, but keep in mind that it came out just before Pioneer Day in Utah so it was briefly pretty popular. After a few weeks though, it sunk a little lower because I don’t think too many wards will be singing it “off-season.”

What hymns are most popular each week?

It’s been long enough that we can get an idea of which hymns were popular when. The following plot shows the percentage of wards that sang each hymn of the wards that sang any fourth batch hymns.

The most obvious trend is that I’m a Pioneer Too (#1034) saw a big spike at the end of July, which is the Sunday before Pioneer Day here in Utah. On July 20th, half of wards that sang from the fourth batch sang that hymn that day. The other spikes in the early weeks for two of the hymns were because there were so few wards singing from this batch, so it’s a small sample size problem. But, we are seeing, Oh, How Great Is our Joy (#1033) and I’m Gonna Live So God Can Use Me (#1037) catching on a little bit and becoming more popular.

Conclusion

That’s it for now. So far, it seems like this batch has been less popular. A part of it might be just sheer numbers: it’s smaller than the third batch but still bigger than the second batch. We’ve also had some holidays here and there, like Father’s Day, 4th of July, and Pioneer Day, so those likely take precedent. It may also just be that the novelty of new hymns has worn off, although it’s important to note that almost half of wards are singing at least one new hymn each week. So maybe it’s that we’re still getting used to the hymns in the first batches before moving on to this latest one. Anyway, we’ll see what happens next week!
